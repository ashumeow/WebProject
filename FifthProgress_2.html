China successfully launched its first moon rover mission

Taking a major leap towards its achieving its ambitious space aspirations, China on Monday launched its first moon rover.

China launched the Chang'e-3 lunar probe with the help of Long March-3B carrier rocket from the southwestern Sichuan province's Xichang Satellite Launch Center at 1:30 am (1730 GMT) on Monday.

The moon rover includes "Yutu" (Jade Rabbit in Chinese), which will carry out a survey of the geological structures of the moon. The lunar buggy was given the name Jade Rabbit in a public vote. The name is folkloric reference to the goddess' pet. The probe will also set up a telescope on the earth's natural satellite to survey observe our planet's plasmasphere - a region of cold, dense plasma that surrounds our planet.

Zhang Zhenzhong, the director of Xichang Satellite Launch Center, declared the launch of the probe successful, and said, "We will strive for our space dream as part of the Chinese dream of national rejuvenation."

If everything goes smoothly, the rover will touch down on the moon in mid-December. A sfae landing on moon will allow China to become the world's third country to do so, after the U. S. and the former Soviet Union.

China's first moon orbiter, the Chang'e-1, was sent in the year of 2007. Chinese scientists are now mulling the possibility of sending a human to the moon some time after the end of current decade.
